About Our Project

Our capstone project explores the fascinating intersection of viral toy trends and digital consumer psychology, analyzing how social media platforms accelerate the lifecycle of collectible culture from niche hobbies to global sensations. We investigate how microtrends like Mini Brands (2020), Pop Its (2021), Squishmallows (2022), Sonny Angel (2024), and Labubu (2025) rise and fall through TikTok virality and emotional engagement.

Using data from Google Trends, TikTok hashtag analytics, Instagram post counts, Kaggle datasets on TikTok Shop scarcity perceptions, and consumer shopping behavior reports, we've created interactive visualizations that reveal how digital engagement, regional factors, and social media influence shape both collectible culture and modern shopping psychology across different demographics and geographic locations.

Key Findings

Viral Toy Microtrends

Each year from 2020-2025 had one dominant viral toy: Mini Brands (2020), Pop Its (2021), Squishmallows (2022), Sonny Angel (2024), and Labubu (2025), with social media platforms dramatically accelerating trend lifecycles from years to weeks.

Regional Scarcity Psychology

Consumers in Can Tho consistently report the highest concern across all scarcity factors‚Äîpromotion deadlines, time limits, quantity constraints, and fear of stockouts‚Äîwhile provinces like Tra Vinh and Soc Trang show lower levels of scarcity concern, revealing regional differences in digital shopping psychology.

Sustainable Shopping Gap

70% of U.S. shoppers still buy items brand new, with only 17% choosing to thrift and 13% buying from resale platforms, despite the rise of sustainable fashion and secondhand marketplaces, highlighting the gap between environmental awareness and actual shopping behavior.
